    Earning From The Show “South Park”

    Parker and Matt decided to extend the contract with Comedy Central for their project South Park in 2007.

    This new deal included a contract clause which turned out to be the greatest deal in the history of Hollywood.

    The clause stated that Matt and Trey would receive 50% of any revenue generated from any other digital medium except television broadcast.

    Therefore, they would earn $25 million to $35 million every year through the show’s broadcast on Comedy Central.

    However, the digital medium was not huge, and the clause was not used for twenty years.

    But the clause came into life after the rising of video streaming devices like Hulu, YouTube and their income rose exponentially.

    Viacom argued about the clause, but it was of no use, and not only did they get 50% ad revenue, but also they could distribute shows in any digital medium.

    The show was being pirated, so thinking that they could not stop piracy,  they put every episode of South Park on Youtube for free, earning millions through ad revenue.

    Similarly, Trey earned $50 million when Hulu brought the streaming rights of South Park for $192 million. It was the highest deal in history at that time.

    Moreover, he earned $125 million when HBO Max bought the streaming rights for $500 to $550 million.
