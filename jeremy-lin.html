    Jeremy Lin is an Asian-American professional basketball player who has played nine seasons in National Basketball Association(NBA). Playing as a point guard, Jeremy is currently playing with Kaohsiung 17LIVE Steelers of the P. League+. In NBA he signed with teams like New York Knicks, Huston Rockets, Los Angeles Lakers, Brooklyn Nets, and Atlanta Hawks.

    Lin was born on August 23, 1988. Raised in Torrance, California. He is a dual American and Taiwanese citizen and belongs to a mixed-racial Asian community. He is the child of Shirley Lin and Gie-Ming Lin. His star sign is Leo. 

    Moreover, Jeremy Lin was born in a Catholic family in the San Francisco Bay Area of Palo Alto. His parents emigrated from Taiwan to the United States in the mid-1970s.

    Meanwhile, the 31-year-old, whose 2012 valiant New York Knicks years are dubbed "Linsanity," would contribute $500,000 and added that he will also support contributions up to an extra $500,000.

    Net Worth: How Much Does Jeremy Lin Earn?

    Jeremy Lin has an estimated net worth of about $36 million. But he has made more than $60 million from his basketball career in his contracts. He has a long history in NBA before joining the CBA. 

    After he was drafted from the New York Knicks to Houston Rockets, he signed a massive $25 million contract for 3 years.  Later on, he was transferred to Charlotte for 2 years after signing a $4.47 million worth contract. 

    Moreover, In 2016, considering his performance and consistency NBA giant team Brooklyn Nets signed Jeremy for 3 years with a jaw-dropping contract valued at $38.3 million. Meanwhile, while Brooklyn renewed his contract in 2018 he received $12.5 million.

    According to Spotarc, Jeremy Lin has appeared for a total of 9 seasons in the NBA, and perhaps by accumulating the contract amount he has earned a staggering $64.49 million in total. 

    On the other hand, talking about his salary then, we must have to say he was one of the highest-earning players in the NBA just like Sarunas Jackson.

    Sponsorship And Endorsement Deals Of Jeremy

    Even though Jeremy received so many offers for endorsements, he declined several of the endorsement packages. He claimed that, at the height of his success, he declined hundreds of millions of dollars in sponsorship offers. In one of his interviews, he stated that;

     "I guess my intention is to play basketball, play well, and play for the glory of God," he said. Lin found the basketball company "definitely secondary to my primary employment."

    To now, he has sponsored Volvo, Nike (2010-14), Steiner Sports, and since 2014, he is still the famous face of Adidas. Through these endorsement deals, he must've gained millions of dollars.

    Why did Jeremy Leave the NBA?

    Jeremy Lin left the NBA in August 2019 and signed with the Beijing Ducks in the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA). According to him, race played a part in his decision.

    In his pursuit of a comeback in the NBA, Jeremy Lin made his way back to the court in 2021 as a part of the Warriors' development team. 

    However, he eventually decided to return to the CBA, where he initially played for the Ducks and later joined the Guangzhou Loong Lions.

    Now he plays for the Kaohsiung 17LIVE Steelers of the P. League+.
