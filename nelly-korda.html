    Nelly Korda- Biography

She competed in a number of tournaments, including the ANA Inspiration, Women’s PGA Championship, US Women’s Open, Women’s British Open, and Evian Championship. Nelly won the Harter Hall Invitational, the AJGA Rolex Junior All American, and the PING Invitational as an amateur in 2015. She is currently leading the matches at the Women’s PGA Championship 2021, where her course is dubbed Highlands Course.

    Nelly Korda’s Professional Career

    Nelly began her career as an amateur and competed in the 2015 US Junior Solheim Cup. The next year, she launched her professional career on the Symetra Tour. She triumphed over Wichanee Meechai in the Sioux Falls Great LIFE Challenge. In this manner, she obtained her LPGA Tour Card in 2017. On October 28, 2018, she defeated Minjee Lee in the Swinging Skirts LPGA Taiwan Championship. The next year, she competed in the ISPS Handa Women’s Australian Open against Jin Young, as well as a playoff against Minjee Lee and Caroline Masson at the Taiwan Swinging Skirts LPGA.

    On September 22, 2019, she competed in the Lacoste Ladies Open de France on the Ladies European Tour against Celine Boutier. She competed in an LPGA Tour playoff at ANA Inspiration in 2020 against Brooke Henderson and Mirim Lee. The following year, she won the Gainbridge LPGA at Boca Rio against Lydia Ko and Lexi Thompson. She was spotted competing against Leona Maguire in the Meijer LPGA Classic on June 20, 2021. She recently created history by winning the gold medal in the golf event at the Tokyo Olympics. With this victory, she became only the second woman from the United States to earn a gold medal since 1900.
